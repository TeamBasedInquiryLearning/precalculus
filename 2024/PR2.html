    Section 1.2 Quadratic Models and Meanings (PR2)

    Objectives

    • Use quadratic models to solve an application problem and establish conclusions.

    Activity 1.2.1.

    A water balloon is tossed vertically from a fifth story window. It’s height \(h(t)\text{,}\) in meters, at a time \(t\text{,}\) in seconds, is modeled by the function
    \begin{align*} h(t)=-5t^2+20t+25 \amp \end{align*}
    (a)
    Complete the following table.
    (b)
    Explain why \(h(t)\) is not a linear function.
    (c)
    What is the meaning of \(h(0)=25\text{?}\)
    1. the initial height of the water balloon is 25 meters.
    2. the water balloon reaches a maximum height of 25 meters.
    3. the water balloon hits the ground after 25 seconds.
    4. the water balloon travels 25 meters before hitting the ground.
    (d)
    Find the vertex of the quadratic function.
    1. \(\displaystyle (0,25)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (2,45)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle (5,0)\)
    4. \(\displaystyle (1,40)\)
    (e)
    What is the meaning of the vertex?
    1. The water balloon reaches a maximum height of 25 meters at the start.
    2. After 2 seconds, the water balloon reaches a maximum height of 45 meters.
    3. After 5 seconds, the water balloon reaches a maximum height.
    4. After 1 second, the water balloon reaches a maximum height of 40 meters.

    Activity 1.2.3.

    The population of a small city is given by the function \(P(t)=-50t^2+1200t+32000\text{,}\) where \(t\) is the number of years after 2015.
    (a)
    When will the population of the city reach a maximum?
    1. \(\displaystyle 2020\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2022\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 2025\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 2027\)
    (b)
    Determine when the population of the city is increasing and when it is decreasing.
    (c)
    When will the population of the city reach 36,000 people?
    1. \(\displaystyle 2019\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2025\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 2027\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 2035\)

    Activity 1.2.4.

    The unit price of an item affects its supply and demand. That is, if the unit price goes up, the demand for the item will usually decrease. For example, an online streaming service currently has 84 million subscribers at a monthly charge of $6. Market research has suggested that if the owners raise the price to $8, they would lose 4 million subscribers. Assume that subscriptions are linearly related to the price.
    (a)
    Which of the following represents a linear function which relates the price of the streaming service \(p\) to the number of subscribers \(Q\text{?}\)
    1. \(\displaystyle Q(p)=-2p\)
    2. \(\displaystyle Q(p)=-2p+84\)
    3. \(\displaystyle Q(p)=-2p-4\)
    4. \(\displaystyle Q(p)=-2p+96\)
    (b)
    Using the fact that Revenue \(=pQ\text{,}\) which of the following represents the Revenue \(R\) in terms of the price \(p\text{.}\)
    1. \(\displaystyle R(p)=-2p^2\)
    2. \(\displaystyle R(p)=-2p^2+84p\)
    3. \(\displaystyle R(p)=-2p^2-4p\)
    4. \(\displaystyle R(p)=-2p^2+96p\)
    (c)
    What price should the streaming service charge for a monthly subscription to maximize their revenue?
    1. \(\displaystyle \$10\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \$19.50\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \$24\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \$28.25\)
    (d)
    How many subscribers would the company have at this price?
    1. 39.5 million
    2. 48 million
    3. 57 million
    4. 76 million
