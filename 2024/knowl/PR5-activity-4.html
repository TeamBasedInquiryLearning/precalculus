Activity 4.5.5.

Let’s refer back to the graphs in Activity 4.5.3 and look at the equations of those polynomial functions. Let’s apply Definition 4.5.4 to see if we can determine how the degree and leading coefficients of those graphs affect their end behavior.
  • Graph A: \(f(x)=-11x^3+32+42x^2+x^4-64x\)
  • Graph B: \(g(x)=2x^2+3-4x^3\)
  • Graph C: \(h(x)=x^7+2x^3-5x^2+2\)
  • Graph D: \(j(x)=3x^2-2x^4-5\)
(a)
What is the degree and leading coefficient of Graph A?
  1. Degree: \(-64\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(4\)
  2. Degree: \(4\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(0\)
  3. Degree: \(1\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(-64\)
  4. Degree: \(4\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(1\)
(b)
What is the degree and leading coefficient of Graph B?
  1. Degree: \(3\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(-4\)
  2. Degree: \(-4\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(3\)
  3. Degree: \(2\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(3\)
  4. Degree: \(3\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(4\)
(c)
What is the degree and leading coefficient of Graph C?
  1. Degree: \(-5\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(2\)
  2. Degree: \(0\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(7\)
  3. Degree: \(-5\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(3\)
  4. Degree: \(7\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(1\)
(d)
What is the degree and leading coefficient of Graph D?
  1. Degree: \(-2\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(4\)
  2. Degree: \(3\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(2\)
  3. Degree: \(-2\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(4\)
  4. Degree: \(-5\text{;}\) Leading Coefficient: \(4\)
(e)
