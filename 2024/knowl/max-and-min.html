Definition 1.5.17.

When graphing polynomial functions, you may notice that these functions have some "hills" and "valleys." These characteristics of the graph are known as the local maxima and local minima of the graph. The maximum value of a graph is the point where the \(y \)-coordinate has the largest value. The minimum value is the point on the graph where the \(y \)-coordinate has the smallest value. The local maxima is the highest \(y\)-value in that region of the graph. The local minima is the lowest \(y\)-value in that region of the graph.A graph, therefore, can have many local maxima/minima.
We can estimate where these local maxima and minima occur by looking at other characteristics, such as multiplicities and end behavior.
