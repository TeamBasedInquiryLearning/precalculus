Activity 1.6.2.

Consider the rational function
\begin{equation*} r(x) = \frac{x^2-4x-5}{x^2+x-6}\text{.} \end{equation*}
(a)
Find \(r(1) \text{,}\) \(r(2) \text{,}\) and \(r(3)\text{.}\)
(b)
One answer above gave us some information about the domain of \(r(x)\text{.}\) Which one? Why?
(c)
Another answer gave us some information about the zeros of \(r(x)\text{.}\) Which one? Why?
(d)
Another answer gave us some information about a point on the graph of \(r(x)\) that is not a zero. Which one? Why?
