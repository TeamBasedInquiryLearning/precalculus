Activity 4.6.20.

Suppose you are given a function \(r(x) = \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\text{,}\) and you know that \(p(3) = 0\) and \(q(3) = 0\text{.}\) What can you conlude about the function \(r(x)\) at \(x = 3\text{?}\)
  1. \(r(x)\) has a hole at \(x=3\text{.}\)
  2. \(r(x)\) has an asymptote at \(x=3\text{.}\)
  3. \(r(x)\) has either a hole or an asymptote at \(x=3\text{.}\)
  4. \(r(x)\) has neither a hole nor an asymptote at \(x=3\text{.}\)
