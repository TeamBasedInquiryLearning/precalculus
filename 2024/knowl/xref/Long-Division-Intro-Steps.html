Activity 4.3.3.

Now we will simplify using long division, given \(f(x)=\frac{3x^2+11x-4}{x+4}\text{.}\)
(a)
What is the result when \(3x^2\) is divided by \(x\text{?}\) Place this number at the top left when doing long division.
  1. \(\displaystyle 3\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 3x\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 3x^3\)
(b)
Next, multiply this result by \(x+4\) and write this below \(3x^2+11x-4 \text{.}\) Be sure to line up like terms in the same column. What is the result?
  1. \(\displaystyle 3x^2+12x\)
  2. \(\displaystyle -3x^2-12x\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 3x^3+12x\)
(c)
Now subtract the like terms by placing them in the corresponding columns.
  1. \(\displaystyle -3x^2-x-4\)
  2. \(\displaystyle x+4\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 23x-4\)
  4. \(\displaystyle -x-4\)
(d)
Next, divide the first term in the previous result by \(x\text{.}\) Write the answer on the top beside the first answer. What is the expression at the top now?
  1. \(\displaystyle 3x^2-1\)
  2. \(\displaystyle -3x+1\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 3x-1\)
  4. \(\displaystyle 3x+1\)
(e)
Multiply this resulting term by \(x+4\) and write that answer in the proper columns of like terms. What is this result?
  1. \(\displaystyle -x-4\)
  2. \(\displaystyle x+4\)
(f)
Subtract the like terms. This is the remainder. What is the remainder?
  1. \(\displaystyle 2x+8\)
  2. \(\displaystyle -x-4\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 0\)
(g)
What is the quotient?
  1. \(\displaystyle 3x^2+11x-4\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 3x-1\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 0\)
  4. \(\displaystyle x+4\)
(h)
How can you check your answer? (Hint: Think of regular long division with positive integers.)
(i)
How does your answer for the quotient and remainder compare to the simplifying by factoring method?
