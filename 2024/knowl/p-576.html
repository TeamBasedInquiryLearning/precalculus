Just as with the vertex form of a quadratic, we can use the standard form of a quadratic to find the axis of symmetry and the vertex by using the values of \(a, b \text{,}\) and \(c \text{.}\) Given the standard form of a quadratic, the axis of symmetry is \(x=\frac {-b}{2a}\) and has a vertex at the point \((\frac{-b}{2a},f(\frac{-b}{2a}))\text{.}\)
