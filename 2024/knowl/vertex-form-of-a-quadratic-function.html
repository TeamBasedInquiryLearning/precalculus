Definition 4.1.5.

The maximum or minimum of a quadratic function is also known as its vertex. The vertex form of a quadratic function is given by \(f(x)=a(x-h)^2+k\text{,}\) where \((h, k)\) is the vertex of the parabola.
Vertex form can be used to identify the axis of symmetry, also known as the line of symmetry (the line that makes the shape of an object symmetrical). For a quadratic function, the axis of symmetry always passes through the vertex \((h,k)\) and so \(x = h\) is the axis of symmetry.
