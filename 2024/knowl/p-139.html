Our goal is to figure out how far away Jalen lives from the store. To help us get there, write an equation relating \(d_b\) and \(d_c\text{.}\)
  1. The distance he traveled by bike is the same as the distance he traveled by car, so \(d_b = d_c\)
  2. The distance he traveled by bike took longer than the distance he traveled by car, so \(d_b + \frac{1}{2} = d_c + \frac{1}{5}\)
  3. The distance, \(d\text{,}\) between his house and the grocery store is sum of the distance he traveled on his bike and the distance he traveled in the car, so \(d_b + d_c = d\text{.}\)
  4. The distance, \(d\text{,}\) between his house and the grocery store is sum of the difference he traveled on his bike and the distance he traveled in the car, so \(d_b - d_c = d\text{.}\)
