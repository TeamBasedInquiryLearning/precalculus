Remark 4.5.8.

Refer back to Activity 4.5.4 and Activity 4.5.7. Notice that when the graph crosses the \(x \)-axis at the zero, the mulitplicity of that zero is odd. When the graph touches the \(x \)-axis at the zero, the multiplicity is even. In other words, factors of \(f(x)\) with odd exponents will cross the \(x \)-axis and factors of \(f(x)\) with even exponents will touch the \(x\)-axis.
