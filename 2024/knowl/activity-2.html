Activity 1.1.3.

Solve the linear equation.
\begin{equation*} \frac{2}{3}x-8=\frac{5x+1}{6} \end{equation*}
(a)
Which equation is equivalent to \(\frac{2}{3}x-8=\frac{5x+1}{6}\) but does not contain any fractions?
  1. \(\displaystyle 12x-48=15x+3\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 3x-24=10x+2\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 4x-8=5x+1\)
  4. \(\displaystyle 4x-48=5x+1\)
(b)
Use the simplified equation from part (a) to solve \(\frac{2}{3}x-8=\frac{5x+1}{6}\text{.}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle x=-17\)
  2. \(\displaystyle x=-\frac{26}{7}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle x=-9\)
  4. \(\displaystyle x=-49\)
