Activity 1.2.9.

Ammie’s favorite snack to share with friends is candy salad, which is a mixture of different types of candy. Today she chooses to mix Nerds Gummy Clusters, which cost $8.38 per pound, and Starburst Jelly Beans, which cost $7.16 per pound. If she makes seven pounds of candy salad and spends a total of $55.61, how many pounds of each candy did she buy?
(a)
There are two "totals" in this situation: the total weight (in pounds) of candy Ammie bought and the total amount of money (in dollars) Ammie spent. Let’s begin with the total weight. If we let \(N\) represent the pounds of Nerds Gummy Clusters and \(S\) represent the pounds of Starburst Jelly Beans, which of the following equations can represent the total weight?
  1. \(\displaystyle N-S=7\)
  2. \(\displaystyle NS=7\)
  3. \(\displaystyle N+S=7\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \frac{N}{S}=7\)
(b)
Which expressions represent the amount she spent on each candy? Again, we will let \(N\) represent the pounds of Nerds Gummy Clusters and \(S\) represent the pounds of Starburst Jelly Beans.
  1. \(N\) spent on Nerds Gummy Clusters; \(S\) spent on Starburst Jelly Beans
  2. \(8.38N\) spent on Nerds Gummy Clusters; \(7.16S\) spent on Starburst Jelly Beans
  3. \(8.38 + N\) spent on Nerds Gummy Clusters; \(7.16 + S\) spent on Starburst Jelly Beans
  4. \(8.38 - N\) spent on Nerds Gummy Clusters; \(7.16 - S\) spent on Starburst Jelly Beans
(c)
Now we focus on the total cost. Which of the following equations can represent the total amount she spent?
  1. \(\displaystyle N+S=55.61\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 8.38N+7.16S=55.61\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 8.38+ N + 7.16 + S=55.61\)
  4. \(\displaystyle 8.38- N + 7.16 - S=55.61\)
(d)
We are almost ready to solve, but we have two variables in our weight equation and our cost equation. We will get the cost equation to one variable by using the weight equation as a substitution. Which of the following is a way to express one variable in terms of the other? (Hint: More than one answer may be correct here!)
  1. If \(N\) is the total weight of the Nerds Gummy Clusters, then \(7-N\) could represent the weight of the Starburst Jelly Beans.
  2. If \(N\) is the total weight of the Nerds Gummy Clusters, then \(7+N\) could represent the weight of the Starburst Jelly Beans.
  3. If \(S\) is the total weight of the Starburst Jelly Beans, then \(7-S\) could represent the weight of the Nerds Gummy Clusters.
  4. If \(S\) is the total weight of the Starburst Jelly Beans, then \(7+S\) could represent the weight of the Nerds Gummy Clusters.
(e)
Plug your expressions in to the total cost equation. (Hint: More than one of these may be correct!)
  1. \(\displaystyle 8.38N+7.16(7-N)=55.61\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 8.38S+7.16(7-S)=55.61\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 8.38(7-N)+7.16N=55.61\)
  4. \(\displaystyle 8.38(7-S)+7.16S=55.61\)
(f)
Now solve for \(N\) and \(S\text{,}\) and put your answer in the context of the problem.
  1. Ammie bought \(2.5\) lbs of Nerds Gummy Clusters and \(4.5\) lbs of Starburst Jelly Beans.
  2. Ammie bought \(3.5\) lbs of Nerds Gummy Clusters and \(3.5\) lbs of Starburst Jelly Beans.
  3. Ammie bought \(4.5\) lbs of Nerds Gummy Clusters and \(2.5\) lbs of Starburst Jelly Beans.
  4. Ammie bought \(5.5\) lbs of Nerds Gummy Clusters and \(1.5\) lbs of Starburst Jelly Beans.
